Vetted AI tools directory

2:00 – 2:12

A living directory of tools that have passed Stage 2 review reduces shadow AI by giving employees somewhere to look before defaulting to whatever they already know. This is a starter template — each organisation populates and maintains its own version with named reviewers and mandatory review dates.

Curation logic: A vetted tools directory is only credible if it has three things: clear vetting criteria, a named reviewer per tool, and a published review cadence. Without all three, it becomes a stale list that people ignore within two quarters.

Governance policy reference card

2:12 – 2:20

The one-page governance reference card is your tangible takeaway from this session. It covers the four parts of a working governance framework in a format that can be completed and distributed to every people manager in your organisation.

Governance reference card preview — insert printed card image or QR code link here (recommended: 920 × 140 px)
The card covers
  • Part 1 — Founding principles and named governance owners
  • Part 2 — Four-stage lifecycle with an owner named per stage
  • Part 3 — Green lights, red lines, and HR guardrails checklist
  • Part 4 — Human-first charter commitments and sign-off block
How to use it
  • Complete one card per business unit or team
  • Distribute to all people managers — not HR only
  • Retain a signed copy with HR and Legal
  • Review, update, and reissue every quarter — at minimum

The close — your 30-day commitment

2:20 – 2:30
Facilitator note: Do not end with a summary slide. End with commitment. Name the three most common non-actions people leave with: doing more research, scheduling another meeting, waiting for IT to take the lead. Then offer a tight alternative.

One thing I will do in the next 30 days

Choose one. Then write the name of someone you will tell about this commitment by Friday. That accountability step is not optional — it is the only part that guarantees something actually changes.

Option A — Name the gap Identify one Red Line in our current practice that has no named human owner. Assign ownership, write it down, and share it with my team by the end of next week.
Option B — Draft the framework Use the governance reference card to draft a one-page AI use policy for my team — Green Lights, Red Lines, and at least one owner named per stage of the lifecycle.
Option C — Kill the shadow Propose a 48-hour sandbox process so my team stops using unapproved AI tools simply because there is nowhere else to go. Define who reviews requests and what an approval looks like.
Engagement exercise 5 — Pledge close  (6 minutes)

Individual. 60 seconds of silent reading and choice. Each person selects one option and writes the name of someone they will tell about their commitment by Friday.

Then invite three or four people to read their commitment aloud. No obligation — but the public statement significantly multiplies follow-through. Close on the last person who speaks, not on a slide.
