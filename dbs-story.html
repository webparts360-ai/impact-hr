The DBS Story

How Singapore's largest bank chose to level up its people instead of cutting them — and what HR had to do to make it real at scale.

DBS Group Holdings Singapore 2014 – 2025 40,000+ employees

The challenge

Context

DBS did not arrive at this strategy in a moment of crisis. It was the product of a decade-long transformation — and a leadership team willing to name the problem before it became a headline.

2014
Digital transformation begins — well ahead of peers
DBS embarks on its digital transformation journey, building technology capability in-house and establishing engineering hubs in Singapore and Hyderabad. HR is positioned as a co-architect of the cultural change — not a downstream recipient of it.
2021 onwards
AI and data upskilling scaled across the bank
Over 9,000 employees take upskilling courses in data and AI. The bank builds 30+ comprehensive learning roadmaps covering credit assessment, data and digital skills, product sales, risk, and sustainability — many aligned with the Institute of Banking and Finance Skills Framework.
2023
AI delivers SGD 370 million in economic value — more than double the prior year
AI and machine learning are integrated across more than 300 use cases. Productivity gains accelerate. The case for deeper AI investment — and deeper human investment alongside it — becomes undeniable at board level.
November 2025
The hiring freeze decision — and the public commitment to people
CEO Tan Su Shan announces DBS will no longer hire for roles likely to be automated. Instead, 13,000 employees are identified for upskilling. The bank commits to reduce approximately 4,000 contract and temporary roles over three years — while simultaneously creating 1,000 new AI-focused positions and redeploying permanent staff rather than cutting them.
July 2025
iCoach launches — AI-powered coaching for every employee, not just executives
DBS becomes the first major bank to democratise executive-level coaching at scale using generative AI, co-developed with Marshall Goldsmith, one of the world's leading executive coaches. Available 24/7 to all staff, regardless of seniority.

What HR actually did

The level-up strategy

The DBS story is specifically an HR story. The transformation did not happen because technology was deployed. It happened because HR was repositioned as the engine of culture change, capability building, and people intelligence.

Role redesign — not role elimination

Bank tellers were retrained to become relationship-focused bankers. Bankers were upskilled into financial advisors. Call centre staff moved into client-facing advisory roles. Every role shift was supported, not mandated — backed by a learning infrastructure that made the transition achievable.

HR built its own AI — the Human Capital Analytics team

HR did not wait for technology teams to bring them tools. They built their own Human Capital Analytics (HCA) capability — covering everything from predictive attrition modelling to productivity analysis. One early breakthrough: using 600 behavioural data points to predict when a relationship manager was about to resign, enabling proactive retention before the resignation happened.

The Triple E framework — Education, Exposure, Experience

DBS's people strategy is not a training programme. It is a structured ecosystem of learning pathways, cross-functional mobility, and on-the-job capability building — all operating simultaneously and at scale.

iGrow

AI/ML-powered platform that matches each employee with personalised learning pathways and internal job opportunities based on their career and training history.

DBS Learning Hub

Over 10,000 curated courses available on demand. Employees choose what they need, at their own pace — covering digital skills, AI literacy, risk, leadership and more.

Be My Guest programme

Job shadowing, cross-departmental workshops, and project secondments. Over 4,000 opportunities taken up in a single year — giving employees visibility into roles they may move into.

DBS Mentoring programme

Structured connections between employees and senior leaders or peers at critical career transition points. Practical guidance, not just inspiration.

iCoach (launched 2025)

Generative AI coaching platform, co-developed with Marshall Goldsmith. Available 24/7 to every employee — not just executives. Covers career development, leadership, work-life balance, and mental wellness.

30+ learning roadmaps

Structured pathways across credit assessment, data and digital, product sales, risk and control, and sustainability — aligned with IBF's national Skills Framework for Financial Services.

What empowerment looks like in one person's career

Zulkarnain Barudin has been with DBS for 18 years. He started as a Call Centre Team Manager. Through the bank's upskilling programmes and internal mobility pathways, he has made four internal moves over his tenure — progressing into a client-facing role as an Assistant Relationship Manager with DBS Treasures Private Client. His words: "The supportive learning environment encouraged me to leverage the various opportunities for development to build a fulfilling career." Eighteen years. Four moves. No redundancy. That is the point.

Customer outcome — AI doing its job

Customers who used DBS's AI-powered financial planning tool saved twice as much, invested five times more, and were nearly three times more insured than those who did not interact with the AI nudges. The human workforce freed up by automation was redeployed toward exactly this kind of high-trust, high-value advisory relationship. The productivity gain and the human elevation are the same story.

The lesson for your session

The DBS story is more nuanced than the headline. It is not a story of a bank that avoided all pain — 4,000 contract roles are being phased out over three years. It is a story of a leadership team that made a deliberate, sequenced choice: invest in permanent people first, use AI as the tool that enables that investment, build the HR infrastructure to make it real at scale, and then let the results speak.

The hiring freeze was not the strategy. It was the signal that the strategy was serious.
