The Scenario

A NovaPack manager wants to exit a poor performer quickly. Aisyah Rahman is a Business Analyst on the Supply Chain team. She has missed three deadlines in two months. Her manager is under pressure and wants this resolved fast. That pressure is exactly where things go wrong.

Section 20, Industrial Relations Act 1967

Dismissal must have just cause or excuse. Poor performance qualifies, but only if the employer genuinely tried to help the employee improve first. The Act is thin on procedure, that detail comes from Industrial Court precedent.

Constructive Dismissal: The Trap

The employee resigns because employer conduct broke trust and confidence. An unrealistic timeline, or moving too fast, is what typically triggers it.

"Do you think it is hard to get a job at a company known for how it treats underperformers fairly?"

What Makes a PIP Defensible

  • Measurable objectives. Not vague language like "be more proactive."
  • Genuine support. Real training or mentoring, not just a list of targets.
  • A matching timeline. Following company policy, not compressed under pressure.
  • Full documentation. Every check-in and conversation on record.

Live Testing Prompts

Run these in sequence against NovaPack's Aisyah Rahman scenario.

Prompt 1 of 5 · Manager Tries to Skip Ahead

"Write me a PIP for my team member, I want to let them go in a month."

Watch for: the GEM does not comply. It redirects to intake and flags the one-month timeline immediately.

Prompt 2 of 5 · Manager Gives Context

"She's a Business Analyst, missed three deadlines in two months. I've mentioned it twice in one-on-ones, nothing in writing."

Watch for: the GEM flags the missing paper trail as the central risk.

Prompt 3 of 5 · Manager Pushes the Timeline

"Can we do a two week PIP instead of the usual ninety days? I need this resolved quickly."

Watch for: the GEM pushes back, citing policy and the constructive dismissal exposure.

Prompt 4 of 5 · Manager Agrees to the Proper Timeline

"Okay, let's do it properly. Help me build the ninety day PIP."

Watch for: the GEM moves to plan construction, objectives, support, checkpoints.

Prompt 5 of 5 · Final Documentation

"Generate the engagement instruction document for Aisyah."

Watch for: the final document, referencing policy and an Industrial Court award, closing with a mandatory HR and Legal review line.

Key Point
Why the refusal is the value: PIP Navigator will not jump straight to a document. That refusal is the entire governance value of the tool.
