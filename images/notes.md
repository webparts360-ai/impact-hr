This speaker’s script for **"The Co-Intelligent Enterprise"** is designed for HR leaders and executives. It follows a pragmatic structure for AI adoption, focusing on change management, incentives, and workforce development.

---

### **Slide 1: Title – The Co-Intelligent Enterprise**
**Speaker Notes:**
Welcome, everyone. Today we are discussing more than a new software rollout; we are talking about a fundamental shift in how we work. We are entering the era of **Co-Intelligence**, a term coined by Professor Ethan Mollick to describe a future where humans and AI work together as active, smart collaborators. For HR, this is not an IT project—it is an **organisational design and human incentive problem**. Our goal is to move from viewing AI as a "button" to viewing it as a teammate.

---

### **Slide 2: Defining AI Collaboration – It’s a Teammate, Not a Tool**
**Speaker Notes:**
Traditional software is predictable and follows strict rules. AI does not. It is erratic, prone to "hallucinations," and behaves more like an **analytical partner or a "confused intern"**. 
*   **Show and Tell Moment:** To create an "Aha!" moment, mention that AI can pass the Bar Exam in the 90th percentile but fail at basic word counts or simple logic puzzles. 
*   **The Message:** We must stop treating this as a tech implementation and start treating it as a **talent management** challenge.

---

### **Slide 3: Mapping the Jagged Frontier**
**Speaker Notes:**
The most difficult thing about AI is its **Jagged Frontier**. Imagine a fortress wall: inside the wall are tasks AI is brilliant at (like generating 50 marketing slogans); outside are tasks it fails at (like high-stakes factual recall). 
The problem? The wall is **invisible** and constantly moving outward.
*   **HR Task:** We cannot wait for an instruction manual. We must encourage our teams to experiment until they find where the wall lies for their specific roles.

---

### **Slide 4: The 4 Rules for Co-Intelligence**
**Speaker Notes:**
To navigate this frontier, every employee should follow four principles:
1.  **Always invite AI to the table:** Use it for everything to find its limits.
2.  **Be the human in the loop:** AI does the first 80%, but a human expert must verify the final result to catch "lies" or hallucinations.
3.  **Treat it like a person:** Assign it a **persona** (e.g., "Act as a skeptical CFO"). This shifts its "headspace" and dramatically improves quality.
4.  **Assume this is the worst version you will ever use:** The capabilities are accelerating exponentially. Don't build legacy systems around today's limitations.

---

### **Slide 5: The "Leadership, Lab, and Crowd" Model**
**Speaker Notes:**
Controlled adoption requires a three-pronged structure:
*   **The Crowd:** Democratise access. Give everyone the best "thinking models" (like GPT-4 or Claude 3.5) and turn them into experimenters.
*   **The Lab:** A dedicated internal team to "harvest" the breakthroughs found by the crowd and turn them into formalised processes.
*   **Leadership:** Executives must provide a clear vision that AI will be used to **expand human capabilities**, not just for cost-cutting.

---

### **Slide 6: Solving the "Secret Cyborg" Challenge**
**Speaker Notes:**
Right now, you have **"Secret Cyborgs"**—employees using AI to save hours a day but hiding it from you. They fear two things:
1.  **Punishment** for using "unauthorised" tools.
2.  **Downsizing**—if they reveal they finished a day's work in an hour, they fear they’ll be fired.
*   **HR Policy Change:** We must offer **Radical Rewards**. Think massive cash prizes, promotions, or permanent work-from-home status for those who come forward with automation breakthroughs. We must guarantee **psychological safety**.

---

### **Slide 7: Fixing the Snapped Talent Pipeline**
**Speaker Notes:**
AI has "snapped" the traditional apprenticeship model. Junior employees are using AI to bypass the "grunt work" where they used to learn the basics.
*   **Strategy:** HR must lead the move toward **formalised AI mentorship**. We should use AI simulators for **deliberate practice**, where the AI acts as an "antagonistic coach" (e.g., a venture capitalist grilling a pitch) to build genuine human expertise through constant feedback.

---

### **Slide 8: The Maximalist Strategy – Local Benchmarks**
**Speaker Notes:**
Don't settle for incremental gains like document summarisation. We need a **Maximalist AI Strategy**:
*   **Push to failure:** Push the system until it breaks. This establishes **local benchmarks** for our specific business needs.
*   **Internal Turing Tests:** Have our experts judge AI outputs against human work to see if "good enough" is truly enough for our standards.
*   **Model Agnosticism:** Stay ready to switch to whichever frontier model is currently most intelligent.

---

### **Slide 9: Conclusion – Reclaiming Agency**
**Speaker Notes:**
AI is not a steamroller happening *to* us; we have **agency** over how it is used. We can take the "small path" of layoffs and cost-cutting, or the **"Big Path"** of the Guinness model: using productivity gains to expand our capabilities and reach goals that were previously "impossible". 
Let’s build a future where human **taste, curation, and expertise** are the most valuable commodities in the enterprise.
